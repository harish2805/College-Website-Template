Teaching Methodology

Corporate Interface:

Good "Corporate Interface" is crucial for any B-School. The corporate branding of the Institute facilitates campus visits by recruiters who desire good students at entry level, and then groom and nurture them to be professional and effective managers in the organisations.
At Matrix School of Management Studies it is an important parameter for brand building and success of its students getting to be industry-ready professionals at their jobs after completing the MBA Programme. The students can thus contribute to the company's products, operations, and growth from day one after joining.
In Matrix School of Management Studies, corporate interface includes a range of planned activities with and within Industry - from guest lectures by senior professionals, case study discussions and development, seminars, and workshops on the one hand, and a 2 month summer internship at an organisation, live field projects, industry visits in Pune and abroad, and other such training. This helps in bringing them closer to real life situations and management challenges therein, which they learn early before they get on with their jobs and progress in their careers.

Role Playing:

Role playing is a useful technique for thinking about difficult situations before they occur, so that you have good pre-prepared responses for the different eventualities that can arise. Role-playing can also be used to analyze problems from different perspectives, to spark brainstorming sessions, to experiment with different solutions to a problem, to develop team work, and help group problem-solving.
Role-playing happens when a group of people act out roles in a particular scenario. The scenario is usually based on a problem that needs a solution, a situation that needs to be more closely examined, or a case or issue that demands a different perspective.
In Matrix School of Management Studies, we believe that the learning process can be enhanced and made interesting by implementing the andragogical technique such as role play and case studies in our curriculum.
